Written at age 30 (part 1) - Progress Report in Mar, 2023

Posted on 2023-03-02 Edited on 2023-05-14 In BiWeeklyUpdate

This title makes me so sad. It reminds me of my time in 2016 and 2017 when I wrote on CSDN blog as if it was just yesterday, but now it seems like I’ve accomplished nothing. Even my plan to participate in programming contests has passed seven years.

I went to 2000

I’ve realized some of my small ideals - I went out and saw the world, and experienced more things. I have become more broad-minded. I also found that I don’t know how to live and often torture myself. These and other things slowly reconciled with me at this turning point of 30 years old.

Living until today, I have summarized a life philosophy: slow down, have a good meal, and take a rest. It’s so necessary to make yourself full of energy, which is more important than just trying to save time.

As long as you want to rest, no one can torture you.

The road is still far.

Life is a slow process of being hammered. In these years, the things at home, work, and interpersonal relationships have made me realize what Jia-ge said, “After the age of 19, you will experience greater happiness and greater sadness.” My greatest joy stayed in the year I was 19, and the pain of things has indeed been increasing since then.

Unfulfilled - I still haven’t accomplished what I wanted to do. I want to seriously do one thing, even if it’s a small thing. I don’t care about other people’s opinions. When I’m on the court, there is no one else. If I don’t care about some things, the damage to myself from those petty things is almost zero.

If you look at my experiences of the past ten years, from a county town to where I am now, I have come a long way. In the next ten years, I still have to and can go further.

Just make something, and that’s it.

As long as you start, you are already ahead of others.

I used to think about how to make money through solidity, but now I really want to learn solidity well. And that’s it.
